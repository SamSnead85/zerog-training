We don't believe in one-size-fits-all security training. Different roles require different depths of knowledge and different applications of skills. That's why we've created three distinct learning paths:

## Learning Path 1: Cybersecurity Foundation
### *For All Employees*

**Duration:** 8 hours | **Modules:** 12 | **Level:** Foundational

Every employee is a potential target—and a potential first line of defense. This path builds security awareness that translates into daily protective behaviors.

### Modules

#### Module 1.1: Understanding the Threat Landscape
**Duration:** 45 min | **CSF Alignment:** IDENTIFY (ID.RA)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize the five primary categories of cyber threats facing organizations today
- Explain the concept of the "attack surface" and how individual actions expand or reduce it
- Identify why human error remains the leading cause of security incidents

**Core Topics:**
| Threat Category | Real-World Examples | How You're Targeted |
|-----------------|---------------------|---------------------|
| Phishing & Social Engineering | Business Email Compromise, Spear Phishing | Deceptive emails, fake login pages |
| Ransomware | Hospital shutdowns, Colonial Pipeline | Malicious attachments, compromised websites |
| Insider Threats | Disgruntled employees, accidental exposure | Access abuse, negligent data handling |
| Supply Chain Attacks | SolarWinds, MOVEit | Compromised vendor software |
| Credential Theft | Password spraying, credential stuffing | Weak passwords, password reuse |

**Key Takeaways:**
- Every click, download, and login is a security decision
- Attackers exploit human psychology as much as technical vulnerabilities
- Your vigilance directly protects the organization

---

#### Module 1.2: Phishing Recognition Mastery
**Duration:** 60 min | **CSF Alignment:** PROTECT (PR.AT)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Identify the 7 telltale signs of phishing emails with 95%+ accuracy
- Apply the SLAM method (Sender, Links, Attachments, Message) to evaluate suspicious communications
- Demonstrate proper reporting procedures when encountering potential phishing

**Core Topics:**
- Anatomy of a phishing email: Headers, sender spoofing, link manipulation
- Spear phishing vs. mass phishing: Why personalized attacks succeed
- Voice phishing (vishing) and SMS phishing (smishing)
- AI-generated phishing: The emerging threat of LLM-crafted attacks
- Hands-on simulation exercise (5 realistic phishing scenarios)

**Key Takeaways:**
- When in doubt, don't click—verify through a separate channel
- Urgency and authority are manipulation tactics, not reasons to bypass caution
- Reporting suspected phishing helps protect everyone

---

#### Module 1.3: Password Security & Authentication
**Duration:** 45 min | **CSF Alignment:** PROTECT (PR.AC)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Create and manage strong, unique passwords using approved methods
- Enable and use multi-factor authentication (MFA) on all supported accounts
- Recognize and avoid common authentication attacks

**Core Topics:**
- The mathematics of password cracking: Why length beats complexity
- Password managers: How they work and why they're essential
- MFA deep dive: SMS, authenticator apps, hardware keys
- Social engineering attacks on authentication (MFA fatigue, help desk impersonation)
- Account recovery security

**Key Takeaways:**
- Never reuse passwords across accounts
- MFA blocks 99.9% of automated attacks—use it everywhere
- Treat password recovery questions as secondary passwords

---

#### Module 1.4: Safe Internet & Email Practices
**Duration:** 45 min | **CSF Alignment:** PROTECT (PR.PS)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Apply safe browsing practices to prevent drive-by downloads and watering hole attacks
- Identify and avoid common malware distribution vectors
- Handle email attachments and links safely

**Core Topics:**
- Browser security settings and extensions
- Recognizing malicious websites and downloads
- Safe use of public Wi-Fi and VPNs
- Email hygiene: Attachments, links, and external communications
- Shadow IT risks: Unapproved apps and services

---

#### Module 1.5: Protecting Sensitive Data
**Duration:** 60 min | **CSF Alignment:** PROTECT (PR.DS)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Classify data according to organization sensitivity levels
- Apply appropriate handling procedures for each data classification
- Recognize and prevent common data exposure scenarios

**Core Topics:**
- Data classification framework: Public, Internal, Confidential, Restricted
- Safe data handling: At rest, in transit, in use
- Secure file sharing and collaboration
- Clean desk and screen lock policies
- Data disposal: Secure deletion and physical destruction

---

#### Module 1.6: Physical Security Awareness
**Duration:** 30 min | **CSF Alignment:** PROTECT (PR.AC)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Apply physical access control procedures to prevent unauthorized entry
- Recognize tailgating and piggybacking attempts
- Protect devices and credentials in public and shared spaces

**Core Topics:**
- Badge security and access control
- Visitor management procedures
- Secure workspace practices
- Lost/stolen device protocols
- Travel security

---

#### Module 1.7: Social Engineering Defense
**Duration:** 60 min | **CSF Alignment:** PROTECT (PR.AT)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Identify the psychological principles attackers exploit (authority, urgency, reciprocity)
- Recognize pretexting, baiting, and quid pro quo attacks
- Apply verification procedures to defeat social engineering

**Core Topics:**
- The psychology of manipulation: Cialdini's principles in attack scenarios
- Voice-based social engineering (vishing)
- In-person social engineering and impersonation
- AI-powered social engineering: Deepfakes and voice cloning
- Building a verification culture

---

#### Module 1.8: Incident Recognition & Reporting
**Duration:** 45 min | **CSF Alignment:** DETECT (DE.CM), RESPOND (RS.CO)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Recognize indicators of potential security incidents
- Follow proper incident reporting procedures
- Understand your role in incident response

**Core Topics:**
- Indicators of compromise: What to watch for
- Reporting channels and escalation procedures
- Preserving evidence: What not to do
- Incident types and appropriate responses
- Post-incident expectations

---

#### Module 1.9: Mobile Device Security
**Duration:** 45 min | **CSF Alignment:** PROTECT (PR.PS)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Secure mobile devices used for work purposes
- Recognize mobile-specific threats and attack vectors
- Apply safe practices for BYOD and corporate devices

**Core Topics:**
- Mobile device management and security settings
- App security and permission management
- Mobile phishing and smishing
- Secure mobile communication
- Lost/stolen device response

---

#### Module 1.10: Remote Work Security
**Duration:** 45 min | **CSF Alignment:** PROTECT (PR.AC, PR.DS)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Establish a secure home office environment
- Protect work data and systems when working remotely
- Maintain security practices across work locations

**Core Topics:**
- Home network security
- VPN usage and requirements
- Secure video conferencing
- Physical security at home and in public spaces
- Managing work/personal device boundaries

---

#### Module 1.11: Cloud & Collaboration Security
**Duration:** 45 min | **CSF Alignment:** PROTECT (PR.DS, PR.AC)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Apply security best practices to cloud storage and collaboration tools
- Configure sharing and access controls appropriately
- Recognize risks of improper cloud usage

**Core Topics:**
- Cloud sharing permissions and defaults
- Collaboration platform security (Teams, Slack, Google Workspace)
- External sharing risks and controls
- Personal account vs. business account usage
- Cloud access security brokers (CASB) and DLP

---

#### Module 1.12: Building a Security Culture
**Duration:** 30 min | **CSF Alignment:** GOVERN (GV.OC)

**Learning Objectives:**
- Explain the importance of security culture to organizational resilience
- Identify personal behaviors that contribute to security culture
- Commit to continuous security awareness and improvement

**Core Topics:**
- The human element in cybersecurity
- Security as a shared responsibility
- Psychological safety and reporting
- Continuous learning mindset
- Personal security improvement plan

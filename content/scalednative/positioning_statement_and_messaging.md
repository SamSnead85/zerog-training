## Tone Guidelines

### Be Authoritative
We are the standard, not a challenger. Write with confidence.

### Be Clear
No buzzwords. No jargon. Say what we mean.

### Be Enterprise-Ready
Content should pass legal and security review. No hyperbole.

### Be Benefit-Oriented
Focus on outcomes, not features. What does this mean for the customer?

### Be Confident
We own this category. Act like it.

---

## Style Guidelines

### No em dashes
Use commas, periods, or colons instead.

### Short paragraphs
2-4 sentences maximum.

### Active voice
"We provide" not "is provided by"

### Concrete examples
Specific, not abstract.

### Scannable
Use headings, bullets, and tables.

---

## Words to Use

- Framework (not methodology)
- Transformation (not training)
- Verified (not certified, unless referring to NATIVE certification)
- Capability (not skill)
- AI-Native (always hyphenated)
- Enterprise (for large organizations)
- Practitioner (for certified individuals)

## Words to Avoid

- Revolutionary
- Game-changing
- Cutting-edge
- Best-in-class
- World-class
- Leverage (as a verb)
- Synergy
- Paradigm
